#404 - Concierge-Style AI Product Intake Experience (Public + Portal Quote Flow)

What & Why

The current quote/category flow still feels too technical and form-heavy.

Even with Product Brain and the classifier implemented, the experience still behaves like:
- “fill fields”
- “pick category”
- “paste URL”
- “hope the AI extracts correctly”

This does not feel modern, concierge-like, AI-native, or emotionally smooth.

The goal of this task is to redesign the quote intake experience across the PUBLIC quote flow and the CLIENT PORTAL quote flow into a more human, guided, conversational product-intake experience.

The experience should feel less like:
“Fill a shipping form.”

And more like:
“Tell CRBOX what you want to bring and we’ll help you understand it.”

This task focuses on UX + interaction architecture + AI orchestration.

NOT on changing calculator math or admin quote logic.

---

CORE EXPERIENCE SHIFT

Move from:
technical form-first UX

To:
AI concierge product understanding UX.

The first interaction should not feel like:
- category selection
- forms
- dropdowns
- customs language
- courier complexity

The first interaction should feel like:
- product discovery
- product understanding
- import guidance
- conversational assistance
- progressive disclosure

The system should help the customer understand:
- what category the product belongs to
- what taxes it usually pays
- whether it may require permits
- whether it may need review
- whether CRBOX can likely bring it
- what information is still needed

WITHOUT overwhelming the user.

---

TOUCHPOINTS REQUIRED

Apply this experience to:

1. Public cotizar flow
2. Client portal quote creation flow
3. Multi-product flows
4. Product URL interpretation
5. Product understanding step before calculation
6. Transition into calculadora
7. Transition into final CRBOX quote request
8. Floating CRBOX AI assistant/chat

Admin/sales must still receive ALL structured information internally.

---

The experience should begin with:

friendly explanation

The user should feel guided by a smart assistant.

INITIAL STATE

Instead of showing category dropdowns immediately:

Show:

Headline:
“¿Qué te gustaría traer?”

Subheadline:
“Cuéntanos el producto y CRBOX te ayudará a entender impuestos, restricciones y próximos pasos.”

Input:
“Describe tu producto”

Examples below:
- “AirPods Pro”
- “Tenis Nike”
- “Computadora gaming”
- “Perfume Dior”
- “Stanley Quencher”
- “Proteína Whey”

Optional secondary input:
“Agregar enlace del producto (opcional)”

CTA:
“Obtener información”

The URL should feel optional and supportive.

NOT mandatory.

---

IMPORTANT CHANGE — URL IS CONTEXT, NOT SCRAPING MISSION

The current AI crawling/scraping approach is too fragile and creates broken-feeling UX.

Do NOT optimize for:
- perfect crawling
- perfect extraction
- perfect dimensions
- perfect scraping

Instead:

Treat the URL as contextual assistance.

The real intelligence should come from:
- Gemini interpretation
- Product Brain
- semantic approximation
- natural language understanding

The system may attempt to extract:
- title
- category hints
- price if available

But if the crawl is incomplete:
the UX should still feel successful and guided.

Examples:

“Ya entendimos el tipo de producto.”
“Detectamos una categoría probable.”
“Solo falta confirmar algunos detalles.”

NOT:
- “No se pudo analizar”
- “AI failed”
- “No match”
- “Extraction error”

TRANSITION TO CALCULADORA

The AI intake should intelligently guide users into:
- calculadora
- or CRBOX quote submission

depending on intent.

---

IF USER WANTS GENERAL UNDERSTANDING

Example:
“How much do shoes pay?”

Guide them toward:
- friendly explanation
- category understanding
- optional calculator

---

IF USER WANTS SHIPPING ESTIMATE

Guide toward:
“Continuar a calculadora”

AND preload:
- detected product
- category
- Product Brain result
- any extracted title
- URL if present

The calculadora should hydrate automatically with this context.

The user should NOT need to repeat information.

---

IF USER WANTS HUMAN REVIEW / FULL QUOTE

Guide toward:
“Solicitar cotización con CRBOX”

OR:
“Enviar a revisión”

The existing quote flow should receive:
- all detected metadata
- Product Brain classification
- customer input
- URL
- extracted title
- semantic approximation
- risk flags
- category
- tax range
- all AI interpretation context

---

IMPORTANT — ADMIN MUST RECEIVE FULL STRUCTURED DATA

Even though the customer-facing UX becomes lighter and more conversational:

CRBOX admin/sales must receive ALL structured metadata internally.

Nothing should be lost.

The admin panel should still receive:
- original input
- URL
- extracted title
- Product Brain category
- legacy category code
- semantic approximation
- estimatedDAI
- IVA
- totalEstimatedRate
- manualReviewRequired
- regulatedProduct
- restrictedProduct
- forbiddenProduct
- riskFlags
- customerMessage
- adminNotes
- actionForAdmin
- extraction source
- any AI reasoning metadata needed internally

The UX can feel simple to the customer while remaining extremely structured internally.

The assistant should feel connected to the quote flow.

NOT like a separate generic chatbot.

→ next-step routing  
→ existing calculator/admin systems
