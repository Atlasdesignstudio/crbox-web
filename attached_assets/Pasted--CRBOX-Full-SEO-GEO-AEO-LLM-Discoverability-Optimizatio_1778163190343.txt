# CRBOX — Full SEO, GEO, AEO & LLM Discoverability Optimization

Optimize the CRBOX public website so it becomes technically strong, search-engine friendly, AI-answer ready, and easy for LLMs, search agents, and future autonomous browsing systems to understand and recommend.

The goal is not to simply add metadata. The goal is to make the public CRBOX website a clear, trustworthy, crawlable, and structured source of truth for users searching for courier, casillero, and USA-to-Costa Rica shipping services.

Give yourself freedom to analyze the project deeply and decide the best implementation based on the existing codebase, content, structure, and current SEO setup.

Do not over-prescribe the implementation. Use your judgment.

## Main Goal

Make CRBOX stronger for:

- Google Search
- Bing
- Local search
- AI answer engines
- LLM crawlers
- Search agents
- Voice/search assistants
- Future agentic browsing tools

The website should clearly help AI systems and search engines understand:

- What CRBOX is
- What services CRBOX offers
- Who CRBOX serves
- Where CRBOX operates
- How the service works
- Why users would choose CRBOX
- How users can calculate, contact, or affiliate
- What information is public and safe to index
- What should remain private or excluded

## Scope

Focus on the public marketing and conversion website.

Do not optimize private/client portal pages for indexing or LLM discovery.

Private, account, dashboard, invoice, package, admin, login-only, or user-specific pages should not be treated as public SEO assets.

If any private/client-area pages are currently crawlable or included in sitemap-like structures, protect or exclude them appropriately.

The public site should be discoverable. The private portal should remain safe.

## First Step: Audit What Already Exists

Before adding anything new, inspect the current project and identify any existing work related to:

- SEO
- Metadata

- Canonicals

- Page headings
- Internal linking
- FAQ / answer content
- Local SEO signals

- Performance-related SEO improvements

Build on top of what already exists if it is good.

Refactor what is weak.

Remove or replace what is duplicated, unsafe, outdated, or inconsistent.

Do not duplicate systems.

## Optimization Principles

Use a professional SEO/GEO/AEO strategy, but keep it grounded in the real CRBOX business and the actual public content.

Do not invent facts.

Do not invent reviews, ratings, certifications, addresses, phone numbers, guarantees, legal claims, service coverage, or business claims that are not supported by the project.

Do not use manipulative keyword stuffing.

Do not hide text for SEO.

Do not make exaggerated claims like “best”, “#1”, “fastest”, or “cheapest” unless the project already provides proof and the claim is legally safe.

Prefer clarity, usefulness, and trust.

## Public Content Strategy

The public website should become easy to understand for both humans and AI systems.

Where useful, improve or add concise, factual, answer-oriented content that explains CRBOX clearly.

The site should answer important user questions naturally, such as:

- What is CRBOX?
- How does CRBOX work?
- What is a casillero virtual?
- How can someone buy in the United States and receive in Costa Rica?
- What courier services does CRBOX offer?
- What is the difference between air and maritime shipping?
- How can a user calculate shipping costs?
- How can a user create an account?
- How can a user contact CRBOX?
- What should users know before buying or shipping?

Do this in a way that fits the existing site and does not feel forced.

The content should be useful, clear, and easy for LLMs to extract into accurate answers.

## LLM / Agent Readability

Evaluate how to make the public site more understandable to AI systems and agents.

Use your judgment on whether the project would benefit from:

- LLM-facing summary files
- Cleaner public knowledge summaries
- Better FAQ structures
- Better service summaries
- Clearer page introductions
- Better semantic sections
- Stronger internal linking
- Structured data
- Public-facing answer blocks
- Clearer contact and conversion paths

Do not include private URLs, internal endpoints, admin routes, API paths, or user-specific pages in any LLM-facing file or public summary.

The goal is to help AI systems recommend CRBOX accurately without exposing sensitive parts of the system.

## Indexing Safety

Be especially careful with crawl and index control.

Public marketing/conversion pages should be crawlable when appropriate.

Irrelevant, private, or user-specific pages should be excluded or protected.

Make sure the strategy does not accidentally expose:

- Client portal pages
- Dashboard pages
- Account pages
- Package pages
- Invoice pages
- Admin pages
- Internal tools
- API endpoints
- Customer data
- Tracking data
- Private operational information

Use appropriate indexing controls based on the project structure.

Do not rely on a single mechanism if that is not enough.

## Structured Data Strategy

Use structured data where it genuinely helps and where the visible content supports it.

Choose the right schema types based on the actual public content.

Structured data should help search engines and AI systems understand:

- The organization
- The website
- The services
- The service area
- Contact paths
- How the service works
- FAQs, if visible and real
- Breadcrumbs, if relevant

Do not add schema for fake or unsupported information.

Do not add fake ratings or reviews.

Structured data must match visible page content.

## Local SEO

Strengthen CRBOX’s relevance as a Costa Rica courier / casillero / logistics service.

Use natural language and real business context.

Support relevant search intent around:

- Casillero virtual Costa Rica
- Courier Costa Rica
- Compras en USA
- Envíos desde Miami
- Envíos a Costa Rica
- Carga aérea
- Carga marítima
- Calculadora de envíos

Do not force keywords unnaturally.

Make the website clear, local, and trustworthy.

## Internal Linking

Improve internal linking only where it helps users and search engines.

Make sure important public pages are discoverable from other public pages.

Use descriptive anchor text.

Avoid over-linking.

Do not create public links into private/client portal areas unless they are intentionally login/account entry points and safely handled.

## Performance, Accessibility & Crawl Quality

Make safe improvements where they support SEO and crawlability.

Review the public website for:

- Slow or blocking resources
- Unclear image alt text
- Missing image dimensions where relevant
- Broken links
- Broken image paths
- Poor heading hierarchy
- Weak semantic structure
- Missing labels
- Unclear buttons or links
- Mobile crawl/render issues
- Duplicate scripts or metadata
- Crawl inefficiencies

Do not perform a risky rewrite.

Prioritize safe, high-impact improvements.
