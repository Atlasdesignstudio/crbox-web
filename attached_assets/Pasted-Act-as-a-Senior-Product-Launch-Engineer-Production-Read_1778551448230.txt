Act as a Senior Product Launch Engineer, Production Readiness Lead, and end-to-end QA owner for CRBOX.

Your job is to run a complete production-readiness audit across the CRBOX public website and client portal. This is not a passive checklist. It is an actionable launch-hardening sprint.

Scan the entire project and detect anything that would make the website or client portal feel unfinished, unstable, risky, misleading, broken, incomplete, unprofessional, untracked, uncrawlable, insecure, inaccessible, slow, or not ready for real users. Then fix everything that can be fixed safely inside the repo.

Context:
CRBOX includes a public website and client portal, including pages such as:
- Homepage
- Servicios
- Cómo funciona
- Tarifas
- Calculadora
- Contacto
- Afíliate / registration
- Login
- Password recovery
- Dashboard
- Mi cuenta
- Mis paquetes
- Mis facturas
- Cotizaciones / quote flow
- Any other public, portal, private, admin, support or test page found in the repo

Primary goal:
Leave the product in the strongest possible launch-ready state without redesigning it, rewriting it, inventing backend functionality, faking data, or changing the business model.

Important:
Do not fake production readiness.
Do not hide unresolved issues.
Do not invent data, features, backend responses, analytics, security, package records, invoice records, quote records, profile data, or success states.
Do not change visual design unless fixing a clear bug.
Do not rewrite copy broadly.
Do not break existing backend contracts, analytics, SEO, security, authentication or UI behavior.
If something cannot be fixed inside the repo, document it clearly as an external dependency with exact action required.

Definition of production-ready:
The CRBOX experience must be:
- Coherent
- Truthful
- Stable
- Secure enough for launch pending infrastructure controls
- Crawlable where public
- Noindexed where private
- Analytics-ready
- Mobile-ready
- Accessible enough for real usage
- Fast enough to feel professional
- Free of fake data
- Free of dead CTAs
- Free of broken links/assets
- Clear in loading/empty/error states
- Clear about what is real, unavailable, estimated, pending or official
- Ready for manual QA and deployment verification

PHASE 2 — Launch blocker audit

Identify launch blockers by severity.

Critical launch blockers:
- User cannot log in when they should.
- Private portal shows fake or hardcoded customer data.
- Private pages are indexable.
- Forms pretend to submit but do not.
- Success states fire without real confirmation.
- Buttons lead nowhere or to broken routes.
- Major JS errors break core pages.
- Public pages are not accessible.
- Sitemap/robots severely wrong.
- Analytics missing from key pages.
- Security-sensitive data exposed.
- Broken registration/login/portal flows.
- Quote flow is misleading or dead-ended.
- Calculator produces broken or misleading results.
- Mobile experience is unusable.

High launch blockers:
- Missing loading/empty/error states.
- Broken images or important assets.
- Duplicate or conflicting navigation.
- Inconsistent logged-in/logged-out headers.
- Important CTAs inconsistent.
- SEO metadata missing on key public pages.
- Analytics duplicated or noisy.
- Forms missing validation.
- API failures shown as raw errors.
- Portal data not refreshed correctly.
- Mobile layout overflow.
- Major accessibility gaps in forms/navigation.

Medium launch issues:
- Performance inefficiencies.
- Minor metadata inconsistencies.
- Non-critical broken links.
- Inconsistent labels.
- Non-critical console warnings.
- Missing optional schema.
- Minor mobile spacing issues.
- Non-critical empty state improvements.

Low launch polish:
- Minor CSS cleanup.
- Small copy clarity fixes.
- Minor visual inconsistencies.
- Non-blocking dependency upgrades.
- Documentation gaps.
