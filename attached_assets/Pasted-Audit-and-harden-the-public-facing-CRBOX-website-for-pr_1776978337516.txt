Audit and harden the public-facing CRBOX website for production readiness, technical SEO, AEO/GEO, AI-search visibility, and marketing measurement.

Important scope:
- Include only the public client-facing website pages and public shared assets.
- Exclude login, affiliate/registration flows, dashboard, mis-paquetes, mis-facturas, mi-cuenta, and all client-portal/demo pages from the production verdict. Those are intentionally unfinished demos.
- Keep the current multi-page HTML/CSS/JS architecture unless there is a strong reason to change it.
- Do not redesign the UI unless a change is required for crawlability, accessibility, performance, trust, or conversion clarity.

Primary objective:
Make CRBOX’s public website as technically strong as possible so search engines and AI systems can easily crawl, render, index, understand, and cite it for relevant shipping intent to Costa Rica, while keeping private/demo areas safe and non-indexed.

Critical principles:
1. Do not promise or optimize for “gaming” rankings. Optimize for best-practice visibility, authority, clarity, usability, and measurement.
2. Public pages should be highly crawlable and indexable.
3. Private/demo/client-portal pages should be safely excluded from indexing using the correct method.
4. The site should be explicit, semantically structured, machine-readable, and easy for LLM-based answer engines to summarize and cite.
5. The site should follow strong SEO, AEO/GEO, accessibility, performance, analytics, and conversion best practices.

Deliverables:
1. A concise audit of the current public site.
2. A prioritized list of Critical / Medium / Nice-to-have issues.
3. A file-by-file implementation plan.
4. Then implement the fixes.

What to optimize for:

A. Crawlability, indexing, and safety
- Ensure all public pages return clean, crawlable HTML.
- Create and validate robots.txt for the public site.
- Do not use robots.txt to hide pages that should stay out of search; instead use noindex and/or proper protection where relevant.
- Add XML sitemap(s) for all public canonical URLs.
- Add a proper 404 page and review status-code behavior.
- Standardize canonical tags on all public pages.
- Prevent demo/client-portal pages from being indexed.
- Review internal linking so all important public pages are reachable in a few clicks.

B. AEO / GEO / LLM visibility
- Improve semantic page structure with clear H1/H2/H3 hierarchy, concise summaries, scannable sections, strong headings, FAQs, and tables where useful.
- Add relevant structured data using supported, valid schema where appropriate, such as Organization, LocalBusiness, WebSite, BreadcrumbList, FAQPage where eligible, and page-level schema where useful.
- Ensure all structured data matches visible content and validates.
- Make CRBOX’s entity information consistent across pages: brand name, service area, Costa Rica, Miami/casillero, shipping modes, contact details, branches, and business info.
- Create or improve content blocks that directly answer common user intents:
  * how to ship by air to Costa Rica
  * how to ship by sea to Costa Rica
  * buy in the US and receive in Costa Rica
  * shipping times
  * shipping costs and calculator guidance
  * customs/taxes overview
  * branch pickup vs home delivery
  * prohibited/restricted items
  * CRBOX service advantages and process
- Make important answers visible in HTML, not only hidden in scripts or interactions.
- Reduce ambiguity and make pages easy to quote/cite accurately by AI systems.

C. Performance and rendering reliability
- Refactor inline CSS/JS into shared files where appropriate.
- Minimize render-blocking assets.
- Improve Core Web Vitals and general page speed.
- Ensure important page content is present in initial HTML and does not depend on fragile JS execution.
- Review image optimization, lazy loading, font loading, caching, compression, and asset paths.

D. Accessibility and trust
- Improve landmarks, headings, alt text, focus states, contrast, labels, skip links, and keyboard behavior where needed.
- Make contact information, service terms, and key shipping explanations easy to find.
- Strengthen trust signals on public pages: clear company identity, location/service area, contact channels, hours if available, and policy links if relevant.

E. SEO + local + marketing foundation
- Strengthen metadata on all public pages: titles, descriptions, canonical, OG/Twitter, favicon consistency.
- Make page targeting specific and non-duplicative by intent.
- Improve copy for search intent around Costa Rica courier / casillero / shipping from USA / air freight / sea freight.
- Recommend any missing public pages or content modules needed to cover high-intent queries.
- Prepare the site for Google Search Console, Bing Webmaster Tools, Bing AI Performance monitoring, GA4, and GTM.
- Define a measurement plan for SEO + AI visibility + conversions.

F. Analytics / SEM / measurement readiness
- Implement or prepare dataLayer / event tracking for major public actions:
  * calculator usage
  * contact form submit
  * WhatsApp click
  * affiliate CTA click
  * call click
  * email click
  * FAQ engagement
- Propose a clean GA4 event taxonomy and conversion setup.
- Prepare the site for paid traffic landing-page quality and remarketing readiness without hurting SEO.
- Recommend how to monitor search performance, branded vs non-branded demand, landing-page conversion, and AI-answer citations.

Success criteria:
- Public site is production-ready as a crawlable, indexable, measurable marketing website.
- Demo/private/client-portal areas are safely excluded from indexing.
- Public pages are technically strong for SEO and AI citation inclusion.
- Content is clearer, more explicit, and more authoritative for Costa Rica shipping intent.
- The result improves discoverability, trust, and conversion readiness without redesigning the site.

When done, provide:
1. Audit summary
2. Exact fixes made
3. Remaining recommendations
4. Post-launch checklist for Search Console, Bing Webmaster Tools, Bing AI Performance, GA4, GTM, and sitemap/indexing validation
