#384 - Product Brain Expansion to 700+ Product Rows

What & Why

The current CRBOX Product Brain foundation (#382) successfully established the architecture, category system, compatibility layer, utility functions, and initial 206 product rows. However, the product-level coverage is still too limited for a modern AI-first logistics experience.

This task expands the Product Brain into a truly comprehensive product knowledge database with approximately 700–1000 product rows covering the most common products customers bring through courier services into Costa Rica.

The goal is NOT to create an official customs/tariff database.

The goal IS to create a highly practical, AI-friendly, customer-facing classification and estimation knowledge layer that:
- improves natural-language product interpretation,
- improves category auto-detection,
- improves tax estimate consistency,
- improves regulated/permit detection,
- improves UX quality,
- improves admin efficiency,
- improves AI extraction understanding.

This is still a data-layer task only.

No UI redesigns.
No calculator math changes.
No external APIs.
No backend rewrites.

The Product Brain remains local_estimated and reference-oriented only.

---

Core Principles

This Product Brain is:
- UX-oriented
- AI-oriented
- courier-oriented
- operationally practical

It is NOT:
- an official customs liquidation engine
- a legally binding tariff database
- a replacement for TICA/Hacienda/ArancelNet
- a guaranteed tax calculation system

Customer-facing language must remain:
- human
- friendly
- actionable
- non-technical

Examples:
GOOD:
- “Este tipo de producto normalmente paga impuestos relativamente bajos.”
- “Este producto puede requerir revisión por batería de litio.”
- “El monto final puede variar según Aduanas y el valor CIF.”

BAD:
- “Confidence: medium”
- “HS uncertainty detected”
- “Arancel ambiguity”
- “Classification variance”

Aliases & AI Optimization

This is critical.

The Product Brain should help AI classify quickly and accurately.

Each product should include:
- common English naming
- common Spanish naming
- ecommerce naming
- slang/common wording
- misspellings
- plural/singular variations

Examples:

Product:
AirPods Pro

aliases:
- airpods
- airpod

- audifonos bluetooth apple

misspellings:

- airports

- earbuds iphone
- bluetooth earbuds

---

Regulated Product Logic

Products that often require permits or manual review must include:
- clear riskOverrideFlags
- customerHint
- adminHint

Examples:
- lithium_battery
- sanitary_review
- telecom_review
- dangerous_goods
- customs_review
- medical_review
- food_review
- chemical_review

Customer hints should explain in friendly language:
- what might happen,
- why it may require review,
- and what the customer should expect.

Examples:
- “Este producto contiene baterías de litio y puede requerir revisión adicional para transporte.”
- “Este tipo de producto puede requerir validación sanitaria antes de importarse.”
- “El cálculo automático puede variar para este tipo de artículo.”

Forbidden Products

Forbidden products must:
- remain searchable/classifiable,
- but clearly indicate they cannot be imported normally.

Examples:
- firearms
- ammunition
- explosives
- illegal drugs
- counterfeit currency
- hazardous explosives

CustomerHint example:
- “Este producto no puede importarse normalmente mediante courier.”

---

Data Quality Rules

Do NOT:
- invent fake official HS codes,
- invent official tariff rates,
- pretend this is official customs data,
- duplicate product rows excessively,
- create meaningless aliases,
- use technical/legal wording for customer-facing copy,
- break backward compatibility,
- remove existing categories.

Do:
- prioritize realistic ecommerce/common products,
- optimize for AI understanding,
- optimize for user wording,
- optimize for courier operations,
- optimize for customer support efficiency,
- optimize for future Gemini classification.
