B — Needs specific fixes before cutover.

The remaining blockers are operational, not architecture/auth blockers:

Use this latest state as the baseline for the final go-live checklist.

What & Why
We are preparing for a controlled domain cutover where the new CRBOX public website and client portal become the primary experience at `crbox.cr`.

Important context:
This cutover does NOT mean removing legacy.
This cutover does NOT mean migrating auth/signup/password recovery to a new database.
This cutover does NOT mean enabling RDS frontend flags automatically.

The intended model is:
- `crbox.cr` / `www.crbox.cr` → new CRBOX website and portal UI
- `clients.crbox.cr` → legacy API/client system remains active
- `admin.crbox.cr` → internal operations/admin tool remains active
- auth/signup/password recovery → remain legacy-backed
- profile writes/invoice writes → remain legacy-backed
- RDS read paths → activated gradually only after production shadow validation
- old hosting → preserved for rollback

Goal
Create the final actionable go-live checklist and execution runbook for safely making the new CRBOX site public on the main domain.

## 6. DNS Cutover Execution Plan

Write a step-by-step DNS execution plan.

Include:

### Before cutover
- Export current DNS zone or at minimum current records for:
  - `crbox.cr`
  - `www.crbox.cr`
  - `clients.crbox.cr`
  - `admin.crbox.cr`
- Lower TTL for `crbox.cr` and `www.crbox.cr`.
- Confirm old hosting rollback target.
- Confirm new Replit target.
- Confirm SSL requirements.
- Confirm rollback owner.
- Confirm team communication channel.
- Run pre-cutover smoke test.

### During cutover
- Change only `crbox.cr` and `www.crbox.cr` records.
- Do not modify `clients.crbox.cr`.
- Do not modify `admin.crbox.cr`.
- Do not modify MX/email records.
- Do not modify nameservers unless the entire DNS zone has been fully copied and verified.
- Wait for DNS propagation.
- Verify SSL.
- Verify apex and www behavior.
- Verify redirect from www to non-www if configured.

### After cutover
- Run post-cutover smoke test.
- Monitor logs.
- Monitor GA4/GTM.
- Monitor login/signup/recovery.
- Monitor portal pages.
- Keep old hosting active.
- Do not enable RDS frontend flags during the DNS cutover window unless explicitly approved separately.

## 8. Rollback Plan

Write the final rollback plan.

Rollback triggers:
- Login broken.
- Signup broken with no workaround.
- Password recovery broken with no workaround.
- Portal unavailable.
- Public site unavailable.
- SSL invalid.
- Private pages indexable.
- Severe mobile navigation issue.
- Contact/WhatsApp broken globally.
- High 5xx error rate.
- Security-sensitive data exposed.
- DNS misconfiguration affecting `clients.crbox.cr` or `admin.crbox.cr`.

Rollback steps:
1. Announce rollback decision in team channel.
2. Revert `crbox.cr` DNS record to previous old-hosting target.
3. Revert `www.crbox.cr` DNS record to previous old-hosting target.
4. Do not touch `clients.crbox.cr`.
5. Do not touch `admin.crbox.cr`.
6. Confirm old site loads.
7. Confirm SSL valid.
8. Confirm login works on old site.
9. Confirm public pages load.
10. Monitor for at least 24 hours.
11. Document incident and root cause.

Include TTL expectations:
- If TTL was lowered, rollback should propagate quickly.
- If TTL was not lowered, rollback may take hours.
