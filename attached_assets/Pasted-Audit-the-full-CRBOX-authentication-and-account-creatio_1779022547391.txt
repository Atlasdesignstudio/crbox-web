Audit the full CRBOX authentication and account creation flow.

Focus on verifying how everything currently works in the latest website, without relying on assumptions. Check login, logout, session persistence, protected portal access, personal account registration, and business account registration.

Main goal:
Confirm exactly what is required for a personal or business account to be created successfully, what is already connected to the real backend, what is incomplete, and what needs to be fixed.

Please audit:

1. Login
- Verify the current login endpoint, payload, token handling, error handling, loading state, redirect behavior, and profile loading after login.
- Confirm users cannot enter the portal without a valid authenticated session.

2. Session and protected pages
- Check that all portal pages are protected.
- Confirm user data shown in the portal comes from real authenticated backend data, not hardcoded or fake values.
- Verify logout works consistently across desktop and mobile.

3. Account creation
- Review both personal and business registration flows.
- Identify the exact required fields for each account type based on the current website and backend behavior.
- Confirm what data is submitted, to which endpoint, and what response confirms success.
- Check duplicate email / duplicate ID handling.
- Confirm whether business accounts are truly supported by the backend or only visually present in the UI.

4. Truthfulness and gaps
- Detect any fake success states, fake user data, fake portal data, placeholder actions, broken buttons, or incomplete flows.
- Do not fake anything. If a flow is not fully supported by the backend, show it honestly or mark it as needing confirmation.

Deliverables:
- Brief explanation of how authentication currently works.
- Required fields for personal account creation.
- Required fields for business account creation.
- What works now.
- What is broken or incomplete.
- Fix anything safe and obvious immediately.
- List anything that requires backend or business confirmation.

Keep the current UI/design intact unless a small UX fix is needed for loading, error, empty, or success states.
